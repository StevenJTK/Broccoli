Broccoli

Information

Broccoli, also known as Brassica olaracea is a vegetable that is related with kale, cabbage, califlower and even brussel sprouts. Containing loads of nutrients, such as fiber, vitamin C & K, Iron and lastly potassium. To top of it all off, it boasts more protein than most veggie greens. Below you will find a list of nutrition facts that broccoli provides. We will first however begin by talking about them before we reveal the numbers. Beginning with carbs, it mostly contains of fiber and sugars such as fructose, glucose & sucrose. Do keep in mind the carb content of broccoli is very low. The fiber within helps with gut health and even prevents various health diseases. With protein being the building blocks of every human being, you'll be happy to know that broccoli contains a lot of it and makes up almost 30% of its dry weight. Unfortunately due to its high water content, broccoli does not provide as much protein in smaller doses. Before we provide you with the nutrient list that we mentioned earlier, here are the vitamins and minerals that can be found in broccoli.

The Different Vitamins & Minerals

The vitamins and minerals listed below are some of the most important compotents to a healthy functioning body. Vitamin C is an antioxidant that helps protect cells. These combat free radicals, which in turn help combat heart disease & cancer, among other diseases. Vitamin K1's primary role for the human body is that it aids blood clotting and can be found in of course broccoli, but also sprouts! Vitamin B9 is a very important vitamin as it helps to remove harmful effects on the human body, such as homocysteine. Homocysteine in large quantities can be incredibly harmful. Moving on to potassium, it acts as a binder for cells to help maintain a good fluid level within the cell itself. Fun fact, sodium does the opposite of this, in which it maintains normal fluid levels outside of the cell. Manganese is a very important mineral as it helps the body's connectivite tissue, bones and sex hormones, as well as fat & carbohydrate metabolism, calcium absorption and blood sugar regulation. To top it all of, it is also crucial for brain & nerve function. Lastly we have iron which helps in growth and development, however it is more known for transporting oxygen to blood cells within your muscles. It is also required for hormones.
